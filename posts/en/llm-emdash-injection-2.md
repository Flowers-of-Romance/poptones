# On LLM Writing Style: Em Dashes Are Independent

In the [first article](/poptones/posts/en/llm-emdash-dpo/), I concluded that em dash amplification is not a direct DPO preference but a byproduct of the style register shift toward "structured explanatory prose." In the [follow-up](/poptones/posts/en/llm-emdash-injection/), I injected em-dash-rich style via SFT and found colons and semicolons increased in tandem, claiming a "punctuation sub-register" exists.

**That claim was wrong.** A controlled follow-up experiment revealed that the colon/semicolon increase was not internal coupling with em dashes, but the model learning the source model's (Tulu 3) overall writing style.

## What happened

The previous injection experiment fine-tuned Qwen2.5-1.5B on em-dash-containing responses generated by Tulu 3 (8B). Both em dashes and colons/semicolons increased significantly.

But that data contained Tulu 3's entire writing style, not just em dashes. Whether colons/semicolons increased due to internal coupling with em dashes or simply because the model learned "Tulu 3-like prose" was ambiguous.

I had checked that em dashes and colons/semicolons don't co-occur in the training data (correlation -0.19 / -0.01), but this was insufficient. Even without token-level co-occurrence, if "Tulu 3-style text" as a whole tends to use more colons and semicolons, the model would learn that.

## Self-injection experiment

To eliminate the confound, I **mechanically injected em dashes into Qwen2.5-1.5B's own outputs** and used those for SFT.

Procedure

1. Generate responses from Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct
2. Mechanically replace comma-delimited insertions (", which", ", including", ", however," etc.) with em dashes
3. Collect 200 responses with 2+ injected em dashes
4. SFT with identical LoRA settings, 5 epochs

Critical control: colon and semicolon frequencies are unchanged by the substitution (colon/1k: 12.67→12.52, semicolon/1k: 0.05→0.05). The **only** manipulation is em dashes.

## Results

| Marker | Baseline | SFT (Tulu 3) | SFT (self-injection) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **em dash** | 0.000 | 7.397 \*\*\* | **12.467 \*\*\*** |
| **colon** | 3.603 | **5.664 \*\*\*** | 2.917 n.s. |
| **semicolon** | 0.238 | **2.185 \*\*\*** | 0.231 n.s. |
| bold | 0.490 | 1.102 n.s. | 0.895 n.s. |
| bullet | 0.000 | 0.080 n.s. | 0.158 n.s. |
| heading | 0.000 | 0.086 n.s. | 0.000 n.s. |

All values per 1,000 words. Mann-Whitney U test (two-sided). N=50.

With self-injection SFT, **only em dashes increased** (0→12.5/1k, p=3.3e-20). Colons and semicolons did not change at all.

## Interpretation

### Em dashes are independent from other punctuation

When only em dashes are manipulated, colons and semicolons do not follow. The "punctuation sub-register" does not exist.

The colon/semicolon increase in the previous experiment was the model learning Tulu 3's overall style, not internal coupling with em dashes.

### Training data co-occurrence analysis is insufficient

Previously, I used the em dash–colon correlation (-0.19) in training data to dismiss the confound. But this captures token-frequency co-occurrence, not style-level co-occurrence ("Tulu 3-like text tends to have more colons and semicolons").

Eliminating confounds requires **controlled intervention experiments**, not correlation analysis. The self-injection experiment is exactly that.

### The byproduct hypothesis still holds

The previous article's conclusion that em dash amplification is a byproduct of DPO (not a direct preference) remains valid. There is genuinely no em dash preference signal in DPO chosen data.

What changed is the **mechanism** of the byproduct. Em dashes are not internally coupled to colons/semicolons. Instead, each punctuation element independently accompanies a broader style shift. When DPO selects for a particular style, the individual elements contained in that style are each amplified separately.

## Lessons in experimental design

1. **Cross-model SFT confound**: Fine-tuning model B on model A's outputs injects model A's entire style. To isolate the effect of specific tokens, you must mechanically modify the model's own outputs
2. **Correlation analysis is insufficient**: Zero token co-occurrence in training data does not rule out style-level co-occurrence. Controlled intervention conditions are necessary
3. **Question your own conclusions**: The previous "punctuation sub-register" was a clean story, but one control experiment overturned it

## Limitations

- **1.5B model only**: Reproduction on 8B requires GPU access
- **Naturalness of mechanical injection**: Comma→em dash substitution is grammatically natural but does not cover all human em dash usage patterns
- **Larger em dash amplification**: Self-injection (12.5/1k) produced stronger amplification than Tulu 3 SFT (7.4/1k), likely because on-distribution data trains more efficiently. Whether this difference in magnitude affects other markers cannot be fully excluded

## Reproduction

Scripts and raw data: [Flowers-of-Romance/llm-stylometry](https://github.com/Flowers-of-Romance/llm-stylometry)

```bash
# Self-injection SFT (data generation + training, ~90 min CPU)
python emdash_injection_self_sft.py

# Evaluation
python emdash_injection_eval.py eval \
  --model Qwen/Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct --label baseline
python emdash_injection_eval.py eval \
  --model emdash_injection_self/models/sft_self --lora --label sft_self

# Comparison (3 conditions)
python emdash_injection_eval.py compare \
  emdash_injection/eval_baseline.json \
  emdash_injection/eval_sft.json \
  emdash_injection/eval_sft_self.json
```

Environment: NucBox EVO-X2 (AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395, 128GB RAM), WSL2 Ubuntu, PyTorch 2.5.1+rocm6.2 (CPU fallback)
